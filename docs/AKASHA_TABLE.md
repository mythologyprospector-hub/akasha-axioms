# AKASHA_TABLE.md
## The First Canonical Table of Structural Knowledge

### Purpose

Akasha requires a lawful, compact, cross-domain language for representing reality.
This table defines the first canonical primitives, relation classes, and operators
used by Akasha World, Akasha Discovery, and Akasha Forge.

It is not a domain ontology.
It is a **structural ontology**.

Its job is not to name every thing.
Its job is to name the **types of things and relations that recur across domains**.

This is the first step toward:
- Leibniz's calculable language
- Mendeleev-style missing-structure detection
- cross-domain scientific auditing
- systematic discovery of absent concepts, transitions, and bridges

---

# I. CORE AXIOMS

1. Reality presents identifiable structures.
2. Structures can exist in states.
3. States can change through processes.
4. Processes occur under constraints.
5. Constraints generate patterns of stability and instability.
6. Observers and measurements affect what is knowable.
7. Systems tend toward attractors, equilibria, thresholds, or collapse.
8. Missing structure can be inferred from coherent partial structure.

---

# II. CANONICAL PRIMITIVES

These are the first structural atoms of Akasha.

## A. OBJECT PRIMITIVES

### 1. ENTITY
A distinguishable thing.
Examples:
- electron
- cell
- planet
- symbol
- agent

### 2. SYSTEM
A bounded collection of entities, states, and processes.
Examples:
- atom
- ecosystem
- market
- theorem space
- neural network

### 3. FIELD
A distributed influence medium.
Examples:
- electromagnetic field
- gravitational field
- temperature field
- information field

### 4. BOUNDARY
A separation, interface, membrane, or limit.
Examples:
- skin
- event horizon
- phase boundary
- category boundary

### 5. OBSERVER
Any agent or interaction that produces recorded distinction.
Examples:
- scientist
- sensor
- measurement apparatus
- coupled interaction

### 6. SIGNAL
A transmissible difference.
Examples:
- photon
- voltage change
- hormone
- message
- wave pulse

### 7. MEDIUM
The substrate through which signals or processes propagate.
Examples:
- vacuum
- air
- water
- tissue
- graph structure

---

## B. STATE PRIMITIVES

### 8. STATE
A configuration at a moment or scale.
Examples:
- solid
- charged
- alive
- entangled
- active

### 9. PHASE
A regime of organized state behavior.
Examples:
- liquid
- superconducting
- inflationary
- market panic
- biological dormancy

### 10. EQUILIBRIUM
A state of relative balance or local stability.

### 11. INSTABILITY
A state sensitive to disturbance or prone to transition.

### 12. ATTRACTOR
A state or region toward which dynamics tend.

### 13. THRESHOLD
A boundary point at which behavior changes.
Examples:
- boiling point
- ionization energy
- tipping point
- activation threshold

---

## C. PROCESS PRIMITIVES

### 14. PROCESS
A lawful transformation over time.
Examples:
- decay
- growth
- diffusion
- optimization
- computation

### 15. TRANSITION
A change from one state or phase to another.

### 16. FLOW
Directed transfer across a system.
Examples:
- heat flow
- current
- migration
- data stream

### 17. COUPLING
Mutual linkage between entities, systems, or variables.

### 18. RESONANCE
Amplified response due to structural or temporal alignment.

### 19. EMERGENCE
Higher-order structure arising from lower-level interactions.

### 20. MEASUREMENT
The act or event of state distinction becoming recorded.

---

## D. LAW / PATTERN PRIMITIVES

### 21. CONSTRAINT
A limiting or shaping rule.
Examples:
- conservation law
- geometry
- resource scarcity
- speed limit
- topology

### 22. SYMMETRY
An invariance under transformation.

### 23. CONSERVATION
Persistence of some quantity or informational invariant.

### 24. DISCONTINUITY
A break in continuity, scale, phase, relation, or law application.

### 25. SOURCE
A point or region of generation.

### 26. SINK
A point or region of absorption or terminal flow.

### 27. GRADIENT
A directional difference that drives flow or change.

### 28. NOISE
Variation not presently resolved into useful signal.

### 29. ORDER
Coherent structure with persistent relation.

### 30. ENTROPY
A measure of dispersal, uncertainty, or inaccessible configuration detail.

# IV. CROSS-DOMAIN OPERATORS

These are the reasoning moves Akasha may perform.

## 1. STRUCTURAL ANALOGY
Detect the same relational pattern across different domains.

Example:
- electric potential gradient
- pressure gradient
- information gradient

## 2. GAP DETECTION
Identify missing nodes, relations, or transitions required by local coherence.

## 3. BRIDGE INFERENCE
Infer a missing intermediate concept between two structures.

## 4. ATTRACTOR ANALYSIS
Determine whether system dynamics imply a terminal or cyclic state.

## 5. CONSTRAINT MAPPING
Ask what hidden constraint explains observed structure.

## 6. PHASE AUDIT
Determine if observed contradictions imply unrecognized phase distinctions.

## 7. OBSERVER AUDIT
Check whether missing measurement/observer structure explains ambiguity.

## 8. SCALE LIFT
Translate a pattern from one scale to another.
Examples:
- micro → macro
- local → network
- event → process

## 9. DUALITY CHECK
Test whether two different descriptions are the same structure under transformation.

## 10. WORLD COMPLETION
Infer missing lawful structure from a partially coherent system.

2. unexplained sinks
3. disconnected but structurally analogous nodes

5. hidden constraints
